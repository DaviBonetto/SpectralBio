# Abstract

Zero-shot missense scoring with protein language models is usually framed as a sequence-likelihood problem. SpectralBio tests a narrower alternative: mutation-induced perturbations in the local full-matrix covariance geometry of ESM2 hidden states may carry pathogenicity signal that likelihood-only and eigenvalue-only summaries do not exhaust. We evaluate that hypothesis on three connected evidence surfaces: a frozen TP53 validation anchor, a stronger-baseline BRCA2 augmentation audit against a five-model ESM-1v ensemble, and a performance-blind support-ranked top-25 feasible panel derived from a 15,752-gene ClinVar scan. On the canonical TP53 benchmark, the released pair `0.55*frob_dist + 0.45*ll_proper` reaches AUC `0.7498`, and repeated nested cross-validation places the fixed released weight on a stable out-of-fold plateau (mean AUC `0.7510` versus `0.7485` for re-tuned alpha), arguing against a same-surface tuning artifact. Across the support-ranked top-25 panel, 10 genes show positive pair-versus-likelihood lower confidence bounds, 2 are clearly negative, and the remainder are ambiguous, indicating structured rather than universal transfer.

The flagship result is on BRCA2, where covariance is tested against a stronger external baseline rather than against an internal likelihood branch alone. Adding covariance-aware hidden-state geometry to the ESM-1v ensemble improves AUC from `0.6324` to `0.6890`, a paired gain of `0.0566` with 95% paired bootstrap confidence interval `[0.0131, 0.1063]`, and that gain disappears under covariance permutation (empirical `p = 0.0010`). This turns covariance from a TP53-only internal comparison into a falsifiable augmentation result against a stronger baseline. SpectralBio therefore does not claim universal pathogenicity prediction or clinical deployment. It supports a narrower conclusion: full-matrix covariance geometry contains real zero-shot pathogenicity signal, can improve a stronger baseline in a benchmark-qualified gene, and behaves as a structured, auditable phenomenon rather than a universal law. Code, data, and public audit surfaces are released.
